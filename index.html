üìÖ Career Skills Sessions

Below is an overview of all 15 sessions conducted as part of our Career Skills course this semester:

Session 1: Getting Started with Career Skills

During our first PESE lab with Anubha Mam, she began by allowing us to settle before introducing herself and outlining her qualifications. To break the ice, she assigned a creative task‚Äîcompose a song in English and translate it into Hindi or vice-versa. Each student performed their song in both languages. We also did a short physical activity to re-energize ourselves. She then prompted us to share our expectations from the PESE lab and concluded the session by asking each student to summarize their experience with one word, making it an engaging and reflective start.

Session 2: Basics of Resume Writing

This session started with a bit of location confusion‚Äîit was first moved from BOSCH lab to Gate No. 3 due to a scheduling conflict, but later returned to the BOSCH lab. While waiting for Anubha Mam, Neelam Mam spoke with us about self-introductions and resume basics. Once Anubha Mam arrived, she asked us for our initial impressions of her, then guided us through defining our dream jobs and setting both short- and long-term goals. We wrapped up with a one-word reflection from each student, maintaining the interactive atmosphere.

Session 3: Crafting a Cover Letter

Held in CX-206, this session began with Anubha Mam distributing CVs from our seniors to illustrate formatting and key elements. She explained the distinctions between a CV and a resume and when to use each. We were then asked to write three words describing ourselves on bookmarks. A quick energy exercise followed before we dove into a SWOT analysis activity, using handouts to understand strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats. We concluded with each student sharing a single word to describe their experience.

Session 4: Group Discussion Skills

This session emphasized improving group discussion techniques. We learned how to listen actively, contribute meaningfully, and use effective body language. Mam divided us into groups and assigned trending topics for discussion. She observed our ability to communicate clearly, collaborate, and respect differing views.

Session 5: Extempore Speaking Practice

In this session, we honed our impromptu speaking skills. Random topics were selected from a bowl, and each student had a minute to prepare and two minutes to speak. Mam guided us on how to structure our ideas quickly and provided personalized feedback on delivery, vocabulary, and confidence.

Session 6: Storytelling Essentials

Focused on storytelling, this creative session helped us shape compelling narratives with structure and emotional resonance. We shared personal stories, refined them, and explored how storytelling skills are useful in interviews and everyday professional conversations.

Session 7: Group Discussion - Advanced Level

We expanded on our earlier group discussion practice by tackling more challenging and technical topics. Mam introduced the concept of taking roles like initiator, moderator, and summarizer. We learned how to manage disagreements professionally and keep conversations focused and productive.

Session 8: Domain-Based Extempore

This round of extempore speaking was tailored to our technical fields. Topics included programming, tech trends, and ethical dilemmas. Mam emphasized the importance of being able to articulate technical knowledge effectively in professional settings.

Session 9: Interview-Oriented Storytelling

We focused on the STAR (Situation-Task-Action-Result) method for answering behavioral interview questions. Using examples from our coursework and internships, we crafted stories that highlighted our problem-solving and teamwork. We practiced delivering them with clarity and intention.

Session 10: Simulated Panel Discussion

This session mimicked a real panel discussion setup. We engaged in conversations about industry trends and job readiness strategies. Mam assessed our time management, ability to synthesize others' points, and teamwork throughout the discussion.

Session 11: Technical Communication

We were challenged to explain complex technical topics in simple, understandable language. The focus was on using analogies and avoiding jargon to communicate clearly with non-technical audiences.

Session 12: Creative Storytelling

We explored advanced storytelling tools like foreshadowing, character arcs, and flashbacks. Each student crafted a story using these techniques. Mam illustrated how applying creativity makes our stories‚Äîand by extension, our interview answers‚Äîmore impactful.

Session 13: Case Study Exploration

We analyzed real-life business scenarios in small teams. The goal was to identify problems, propose effective solutions, and defend our viewpoints. Mam focused on our ability to think critically and present arguments logically and clearly.

Session 14: Mock Interviews with Storytelling

Bringing all our previous skills together, this session involved mock interviews. We answered behavioral questions using structured storytelling. Mam provided detailed feedback on our content, presentation, and non-verbal communication to help fine-tune our technique.

Session 15: Reflection and Course Summary

We looked back at our progress by comparing early and recent recordings of our performances. Each student shared key takeaways and set personal communication goals. Mam wrapped up the course with tailored feedback and suggestions for our future professional journey.
